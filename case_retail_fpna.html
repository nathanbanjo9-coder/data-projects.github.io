Business Goal

Provide a clear FP&A view of performance (revenue, profit, margin) while exposing the biggest sources of margin leakage (discounting + returns). The dashboard is designed to answer “what changed, why it changed, and where to act.”

Key Questions

  • What is the overall revenue, profit, and margin trend by year/month?
  • Which customers and products drive the most revenue — and which destroy margin?
  • How do discount levels relate to margin outcomes across categories?
  • Where are returns concentrated (products, regions, reasons) and how much profit do they remove?
  • What is net profit after returns (true profitability), and what levers improve it?

Dashboard Structure (4 Pages)

1) Executive Financial Overview
KPIs • Channel Mix • Customer Demographics
  • Topline KPIs: Revenue, Profit, Transactions, AOV
  • Revenue by Channel + Delivery Method
  • Profit contribution by age group + subscription eligibility
  • Revenue by Brand (quick revenue concentration view)
2) Customer & Profitability Analysis
Top / Bottom • Region • Segment
  • Profit margin % by category (margin strength comparison)
  • Top 10 Customers by Revenue (CustomerID-based ranking)
  • Top products by revenue + active customers by region
  • Segment revenue split for targeting and pricing strategy
3) Sales Performance & Growth Analysis
YoY • MoM • Trendlines
  • Revenue growth % trend by month
  • Profit trend by month and year (performance stability)
  • Revenue by year with growth context
  • Revenue + profit margin combined to avoid “growth at any cost”
4) Returns & Margin Leakage
Leakage • Return Rate • Net Profit
  • Total Returned Revenue + Return Rate %
  • Average discount % vs profit margin (pressure analysis)
  • Net profit after returns (true profitability)
  • Return drivers by product / category / region to guide fixes

Key Findings

  • Growth is strong, but margin must be monitored to prevent “revenue-only” decision-making.
  • Discounting impacts profitability unevenly — some categories sustain discounting, others collapse margin fast.
  • Returns are a measurable profit leak that can be traced to specific products/regions (actionable, not generic).
  • Revenue concentration exists across a subset of customers/products, increasing risk and dependency.

Recommendations

  • Introduce margin guardrails (min margin thresholds) for high-discount categories/products.
  • Create a returns reduction plan: focus on top return products first (quality, sizing, packaging, delivery issues).
  • Track net profit after returns as an executive KPI (not just gross profit).
  • Prioritise high-LTV segments with strong margin and low return rates for retention and targeted campaigns.
