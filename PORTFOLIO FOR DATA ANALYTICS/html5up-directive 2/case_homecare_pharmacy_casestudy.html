Key Findings

  • Strong payment performance: 93.01% paid rate indicates healthy revenue realisation, with ~£4.5M pending at risk requiring collections focus.
  • Operational risk signals: 6.34% SLA breach rate (63,400 orders) and 6% delivery exception rate directly link to customer escalations — process improvement needed.
  • Revenue concentration: Repeat patients drive 68% of revenue — opportunity to implement loyalty programs and reduce churn through targeted retention.
  • Courier performance variation: Exception rates vary significantly by courier, indicating service quality gaps and opportunity for vendor negotiation or transition.
  • Regional insights: Revenue and SLA performance vary by geography — opportunity to replicate best practices from high-performing regions to underperforming ones.

Recommendations

  • Reduce SLA breaches by 1-2% (target: <5%): Conduct root cause analysis on high-risk region + courier + time combinations; audit dispatch processes and implement targeted improvements.
  • Collections on high-value pending orders: Focus on pending orders >£150 to recover £1M+ in short-term cash flow risk.
  • Courier accountability: Rank couriers by exception rate; negotiate service improvements or transition volume to top 3 performers.
  • Patient retention strategy: Build loyalty program for repeat patients; target email campaigns and incentives to reduce 32% new patient acquisition dependency.
  • Operational transparency: Publish monthly SLA/exception dashboard to operational teams with accountability for improvements.

Limitations & Analytical Constraints

While the analysis delivers strong commercial and operational insight, several constraints should be acknowledged:

No Cost or Margin Data
Data Gap • Profitability
  • Dataset includes gross order value only
  • Missing COGS, courier contract costs, operational handling
  • Limits profitability analysis to revenue performance
  • True commercial optimisation requires margin-level modelling
SLA Timestamp Assumptions
Data Quality • Methodology
  • SLA breaches rely on dispatch and delivery timestamp accuracy
  • Does not account for system logging delays
  • No buffer logic for operational exceptions (weather, region delays)
  • Refined SLA model would segment controllable vs uncontrollable factors
No Longitudinal Cohort Tracking
Analysis Scope • Retention
  • Repeat patient rate (68%) calculated at aggregate level only
  • Missing patient lifetime duration analysis
  • No churn probability modelling
  • No cohort retention tracking by acquisition period
No Root Cause Tagging
Classification • Causal Analysis
  • Exceptions and SLA breaches measured quantitatively only
  • Missing reason codes and root cause categories
  • No escalation severity scoring
  • Restricts deeper causal modelling

Future Improvements & Phase 2 Roadmap

To evolve this from descriptive analytics to advanced operational intelligence:

  • 1. Margin & Profitability Modelling: Integrate cost data per courier and prescription type; calculate gross margin by channel, region, and courier; identify high-revenue but low-margin segments. Impact: Transition from revenue optimisation to profit optimisation.
  • 2. SLA Risk Prediction Model: Build logistic regression / classification model to predict SLA breach probability using region, courier, time-of-day, prescription type, and workload density features. Impact: Enable proactive service reliability management through early-risk identification.
  • 3. Cohort Retention & Lifetime Value (LTV): Create patient cohort groups by registration month; measure 3, 6, 12-month retention curves; estimate patient lifetime value by channel and region. Impact: Improved marketing allocation and retention strategy.
  • 4. Courier Performance Scorecard: Build weighted SLA + exception + cost composite score; rank couriers monthly; support vendor renegotiation or reallocation strategy. Impact: Data-driven supplier accountability.
  • 5. Operational Dashboard Automation: Migrate Excel pivot logic into fully modelled Power BI star schema; implement incremental refresh; enable automated weekly reporting. Impact: Reduce manual reporting time and improve executive visibility.
  • 6. Escalation & Support Efficiency Modelling: Analyse escalation drivers; identify threshold patterns linking SLA breaches to support tickets; optimise workforce planning. Impact: Reduced customer service workload and improved resolution time.

Closing: This project establishes a strong descriptive and diagnostic analytics foundation across commercial and operational domains. The next phase would transition toward predictive modelling and profitability optimisation, transforming the solution from performance reporting into proactive operational intelligence.
